ATRIA Bleeding Risk Score Accurate prediction of hemorrhage risk on warfarin is vital to the anticoagulation therapy decision. Based on 5 easily available clinical variables, the ATRIA score reflects the experience of a large, diverse group of patients with atrial fibrillation assembled from community care and followed up for a longer period than prior studies. When collapsed into a 3-category risk score, the ATRIA study risk scheme was able to identify sizable proportions of patients who fell into the most clinically meaningful categories, namely, low or high risk for hemorrhage. The ATRIA study scheme led to improvements in accurate net reclassification when compared to alternative schemes.
