IMPROVE Risk Score for VTE The International Medical Prevention Registry on Venous Thromboembolism (IMPROVE) score predicts 3-month risk of venous thromboembolism (VTE) in hospitalized patients and should not be used in outpatients. Scores derived from seven clinical factors during hospitalization may help us to further understand symptomatic VTE risk. The weighted VTE risk scores showed an association between a higher risk score and a higher observed VTE rate and identified high-risk patient subgroups, including those with higher overall and VTE-related mortality.
